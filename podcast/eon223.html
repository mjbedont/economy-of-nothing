This week: Quentin Tarantino's feet, Rick and Morty's drunk improv era, and Mr. Green's can't-fail business plan.

Mr. Green and Captain Ray Tracy spiral from a tense standoff over a missing mystery box into a full debate on whether Quentin Tarantino would actually be fun to do cocaine with (he would not). That leads into the Justin Roiland/Dan Harmon Rick and Morty fallout, a Wire-quoting defense of their own episode quality, and Mr. Green's pitch of The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck, complete with a half-remembered Ralph Waldo Emerson quote.

Then: Mr. Green unveils his dream business. Peak Male — a Topgolf-style warehouse bar built entirely around Big Buck Hunter, margaritas, and a tagline neither of them can quite land. The Captain is along for the ride, the truck may or may not get branded, and the box is still in the back of the Moonshine.

Plus, the emails segment returns under a new name: Be Careful What You Ask For.
